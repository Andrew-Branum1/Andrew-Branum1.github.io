My Introduction to Coding

Born and raised in Walton, Kentucky, I have always had a knack for computers, gaming consoles, and school. My love for computers and coding began to come into fruition once I attended Covington Catholic High School and enrolled in my first computer science class. Learning about logic circuits, truth tables, and various type of outdated computers sparked a flame within me that I couldn't extinguish. Later that year, I began my first coding class covering the basic principles of Java. Seeing how I could create just about anything I wanted with the keys on my keyboard proved to me that coding was meant for me.

The next year, I enrolled in a college credit Java class and saw just how much one could actually do with code. Through trial and error, my classmates and I trekked through the coursework building our skills. With each complete project, my desire became stronger; I loved the feeling of clicking the "Run" button on Eclipse an no errors popping up. Creating something of your own from scratch and being able to modify it as you please simply excits me. After my senior year, I decided the attend the University of Louisville and major in Computer Science and Engineering to pursue my future. So far I am enjoying every last bit of it and cannot wait to see what comes next. After passing the first two programming classes, I am ready to expand my programming skills to the next level through my co-op experience.

About Me

A resume may take my place for an initial meeting, but it will never be able to truly tell who I am. I, Drew Branum, have been raised along with two younger sisters in a family of 5 with a cute dog of course (as seen above). Some of the things I enjoy to do for leisure include hiking, playing video games, watching Netflix/Hulu, cooking food, literally anything with my dog, playing basketball/inter-murals, making new connections, and bridge-jumping.

I cannot stress how much I love staying active. As a kid, I spent the majority of my days traversing the woods with fellow neighborhood children in hopes of finding unknown secrets. Nowadays, taking my laptop outside in the shade and getting to work suffices. Another key component of how I stay active is basketball and the gym. If I don't go to the gym or play basketball at least once a day I feel like a bum. I have a lot of energy and nothing feels better than exerting it on a hard lift or running a few games on the court after spending countless hours on homework.

If I'm going to spend energy, I've got to make sure I have enough to continue my day after which is where my hobby for cooking began. I was tired of coming home from my daily adventures to two hungry sisters with no food made so I took it u on my own. Now, I can create a new meal to feed my desire at will. I find it enjoyable to be able to create, especially if I can create exactly as I please. Cooking food relates to coding in my opinion. You have to lay out your plan before, list constraints, know WHAT to do and HOW, execute the action, and debug/try again.

Even though this is quite brief, I hope you've at least got a small idea of who I actually am, hope to see you in person!
